The Founding of Apple

The Partnership

On April 1, 1976, Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne founded Apple Computer Company in Jobs' parents' garage. Their collaboration began with building "blue boxes" and evolved into creating the Apple I, a revolutionary single-board computer.

Early Success

The Apple II, launched in 1977, featured keyboard, color graphics, and expansion slots, establishing Apple as a serious company. Jobs' vision of making technology accessible to everyone drove the company's early innovations.

The Pixar Era

Strategic Investment

In 1986, Jobs acquired Pixar from Lucasfilm for $10 million, investing over $50 million by 1991. He transformed it from a hardware company into an animation powerhouse, culminating in Toy Story's groundbreaking success.

Disney Acquisition

Jobs sold Pixar to Disney for $7.4 billion in 2006, becoming Disney's largest individual shareholder. This strategic move reshaped both companies and the entire animation industry.

The iPhone Revolution

Revolutionary Design

Unveiled in 2007, the iPhone combined a touchscreen iPod, mobile phone, and Internet communicator. Jobs' insistence on premium materials and intuitive design created a device that redefined mobile computing.

Industry Impact

The iPhone disrupted traditional carrier models, created the modern app economy, and established new standards for mobile interfaces. It transformed how we interact with technology and each other.
